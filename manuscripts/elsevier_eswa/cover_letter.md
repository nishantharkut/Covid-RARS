# Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit "Protocol-Aware Expert-System Validation for COVID-19 Respiratory-Audio Screening Under Temporal Drift, External Dataset Shift, and Metadata Confounding" for consideration in Expert Systems with Applications.

The manuscript presents a decision-support reliability framework for COVID-19 respiratory-audio modeling. Rather than presenting a single internal classifier as the contribution, the paper evaluates a broad acoustic model bank through participant-disjoint validation, time-stratified validation, strict early-to-late temporal validation, cough-only COUGHVID external transfer, and metadata-only confounding controls.

This framing is aligned with ESWA's interest in applied expert systems: the central contribution is an auditable validation protocol for assessing whether a respiratory-audio screening rule remains credible under temporal shift, external dataset shift, and shortcut-learning risk. The revised manuscript makes the expert-system object explicit: each prediction result is interpreted together with its validation tier, modality scope, and admissible claim boundary. Coswara supports multimodal source-domain and temporal analyses; COUGHVID is used only as an independent cough-only transfer endpoint and is not treated as evidence for multimodal fusion.

The results show why the protocol matters. The strongest Coswara ComParE2016+IS10 fusion model reached AUROC 0.897, while strict temporal validation reached AUROC 0.698. In the model-matched cough-only comparison, Coswara internal AUROC was 0.849-0.868 and COUGHVID transfer AUROC was 0.523-0.543. Metadata-only models reached AUROC 0.964, and symptoms-only metadata reached AUROC 0.932, indicating substantial confounding risk. The manuscript also positions itself carefully against the recent ESWA DNDT/DNDF study by acknowledging its stronger internal metrics while showing that temporal, external-transfer, and metadata-control evidence ask a different expert-system reliability question.

We confirm that the manuscript does not claim clinical deployment readiness or state-of-the-art diagnostic superiority. Source datasets should be accessed through their original maintainers; the manuscript reports aggregate metrics and original figures generated by the reproducible analysis workflow.
