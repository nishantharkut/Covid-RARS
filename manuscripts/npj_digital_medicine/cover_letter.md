# Draft Cover Letter

Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Confounding, Temporal Drift, and Dataset Shift Limit Generalization in COVID-19 Respiratory-Audio Screening," for consideration in npj Digital Medicine.

This manuscript presents a medical-AI reliability audit of COVID-19 respiratory-audio benchmarks rather than a new clinical screening model. Using Coswara for source-domain multimodal and temporal analyses, and COUGHVID only as an external cough-only transfer target, we evaluate how apparent respiratory-audio performance changes under participant-disjoint validation, time-stratified validation, strict early-to-late temporal validation, metadata-only confounding controls, and direct external transfer.

The central finding is that strong internal discrimination can coexist with temporal instability, metadata shortcut risk, and weak external cough generalization. The work is intended to support more reliable evaluation practice in digital respiratory health research, especially for observational and crowdsourced audio datasets.

We believe the manuscript fits npj Digital Medicine because it addresses a core evidence-quality problem in digital biomarker development: how to interpret model performance when benchmark labels are entangled with symptoms, protocol variables, calendar time, and dataset source. We do not claim clinical deployment readiness, state-of-the-art diagnostic performance, or external validation of multimodal fusion.

All authors have approved the submitted version, and the manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. Author disclosures, funding details, and data/code availability statements should be finalized in the submission system.
